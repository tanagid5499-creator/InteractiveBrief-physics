# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Undergraduate physics students preparing for and working through laboratory experiments. They use these pages during short pre-lab briefings and as a companion while collecting data, checking units, avoiding common mistakes, and preparing written reports.

## Product Purpose

This project turns 19 physics laboratory documents into student-facing interactive briefing presentations. Success means students understand why each experiment matters, can connect measurements to evidence, can use formulas and instruments with care, and can write better conclusions grounded in data and uncertainty.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, professor-led. The experience should feel like a careful experimental physics briefing: intellectually serious, readable, and supportive without becoming a manual, dashboard, or decorative landing page.

## Anti-references

Avoid dashboard interfaces, generic SaaS landing pages, scanned-document layouts, worksheet-only tools, teacher scripts, top toolbars, dark mode toggles, print/export portals, excessive cards, decorative hero sections, and cluttered manual-style procedures.

## Design Principles

1. Motivation before mechanics: each lab starts by giving students a reason to care about the measurement.
2. Evidence over decoration: formulas, diagrams, cautions, and interactions must directly support experimental reasoning.
3. Presentation rhythm: sections should feel briefable in class, with a short left navigation and focused central content.
4. Thai-first clarity: Thai prose is the main reading voice; English terms and mathematical notation support it without visually dominating.
5. Judgment through measurement: prompts should help students discuss uncertainty, units, mistakes, and whether data supports the model.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, readable Thai typography, reduced-motion support, keyboard-operable controls, no dark-mode dependency, no hidden teacher-only content in the student surface, and responsive layouts that avoid horizontal overflow on mobile.
